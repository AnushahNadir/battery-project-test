Anomaly Detection in Battery Degradation Trajectories

WHAT CONSTITUTES AN ANOMALY
In battery degradation, an anomaly is a cycle where the measured capacity or derived features deviate significantly from the expected degradation trajectory. Anomalies may indicate: unexpected rapid fade (accelerated aging event), measurement artifact (sensor fault, test equipment issue), unusual operating conditions (temperature excursion, over-discharge), or internal cell events (lithium plating episode, micro-short circuit).

Anomalies must be distinguished from natural variability. Discharge capacity varies cycle-to-cycle due to temperature fluctuations, SOC variation at cycle start, and measurement noise (typically ±0.5-1% of rated capacity). Statistical anomaly detection methods set thresholds that balance sensitivity (detecting real anomalies) against specificity (avoiding false alarms on normal variability).

RESIDUAL-BASED ANOMALY DETECTION
The most common approach fits a degradation model (linear trend, exponential fit, or ML model) to the capacity trajectory and computes residuals: r_t = Q_observed - Q_predicted. Cycles where |r_t| exceeds a threshold (e.g. 3 standard deviations of normal residuals, or a fixed absolute threshold based on rated capacity) are flagged as anomalies. The anomaly score quantifies severity: higher scores indicate larger deviations.

For the NASA CALCE dataset, a residual spike anomaly occurs when the model-predicted RUL significantly overestimates or underestimates observed RUL. An anomaly score above 50 cycles indicates a strong deviation; scores above 30 cycles are worth monitoring; below 10 cycles falls within normal prediction uncertainty.

TRAJECTORY SHAPE ANOMALIES
Beyond point anomalies (single cycle deviations), trajectory shape anomalies capture sustained changes in the degradation rate. A sudden inflection in the capacity-cycle curve (knee-point acceleration) is a critical trajectory anomaly: it signals that the battery has entered a rapid degradation phase. The knee point in LiCoO2/graphite batteries typically occurs when lithium inventory is exhausted beyond the cathode-limited region, triggering accelerated SEI growth and mechanical damage. Detecting the knee point 20-50 cycles before end-of-life is the highest-value anomaly detection task.

BATTERY B0036 ANOMALY INTERPRETATION
Battery B0036 shows 32 anomalous cycles, concentrated in the first 30 cycles (residual_spike type). This pattern is consistent with early formation cycling behavior: during the first 10-30 cycles, capacity often exhibits non-monotonic variation as the SEI stabilizes. The residual spike anomaly score peaks at ~66 in cycle 16. This is not indicative of an acute failure; rather, it reflects the model having been trained predominantly on data from batteries that have already completed their formation phase. B0036's early cycles look anomalous relative to the training distribution's expectations.

This finding is practically important: it suggests the prediction model should be recalibrated or flagged as less reliable for very early cycle predictions (cycles 1-30) across all batteries, not just B0036.

ANOMALY DETECTION METHODS
Z-score on residuals: Simple and interpretable. Assumes residuals are approximately Gaussian after trend removal. Threshold typically set at 2.5-3.5 standard deviations.

Isolation Forest: Ensemble method that isolates anomalies by random partitioning. Effective for multivariate anomaly detection (combining capacity, temperature, voltage features). Does not require stationarity assumption.

LSTM/Autoencoder: Reconstruction error flags cycles that the model cannot accurately reconstruct from the learned normal degradation manifold. Effective for detecting subtle distributional shifts but requires substantial training data.

One-class SVM: Learns a boundary around normal degradation behavior in feature space. Works well with small datasets but is sensitive to hyperparameter tuning.

For the NASA CALCE dataset size (34 batteries, ~7000 cycles), residual-based methods and isolation forest are most appropriate. Deep learning approaches risk overfitting given the limited battery count.

INTEGRATION WITH RUL PREDICTION
Anomaly detection output should modulate confidence in RUL predictions. A battery with recent anomalous cycles should have wider prediction intervals. In a production monitoring system, anomaly detection alerts should trigger human review before automated maintenance decisions are made. The anti-hallucination principle requires that anomaly labels be grounded in measured data, not inferred from external knowledge or model extrapolation.
